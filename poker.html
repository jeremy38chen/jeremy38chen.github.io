Introduction

Ten Point Half is kind of poker game which is simple and easy for players to have fun. The rule is similar with Black Jack. Every player takes turns to be the dealer. If one is the dealer, the others are the players. Each player has the right to hit cards, and the total sum of the cards will be compared with the dealer at the end of the round. However, if the player got over ten points half, the player is busted, and the dealer wins the game.

Rules

If one of the following conditions was accomplished, the dealer wins the game.

  • The player gets over ten points half. In other words, the player is busted.
  • The dealer’s total sum of the points is larger than the player’s total sum of the points.
  • If the dealer got five pieces of cards and the points are less than ten points half, the player gets a “Five Dragon”. The dealer wins the game.
  • If the dealer got two pieces of cards and the points are equal to ten points half, the player gets a “Ten Point Half”. The dealer wins the game.

    • If one of the following conditions was accomplished, the player wins the game.

  • The dealer gets over ten points half. In other words, the dealer is busted.
  • The player’s total sum of the points is larger than the dealer’s total sum of the points.
  • If the player got five pieces of cards and the points are less than ten points half, the player gets a “Five Dragon”. The player wins the game.
  • If the player got two pieces of cards and the points are equal to ten points half, the player gets a “Ten Point Half”. The player wins the game.

Implement

After the basic setup for Internet connection, dealer starts to wait for players to connect. If all the players are connected with dealer, the game is started, and then the event of sending and receiving is going to repeat until the game is ended. At the end of the game, the screen displays the result of the game. Who wins the game? The dealer involves several steps below.

Dealer

Step 1

The menu is displayed at the screen in order to get the input of IP, Port and the number of player.

Step 2

Using the function socket() to create a socket to communicate with client.

Step 3

Inserting some value including IP and Port into the struct sockaddr_in

Step 4

The function bind() helps to associate the socket with a port on your computer.

Step 5

Making good use of the function listen() to wait for incoming connections.

Step 6

Using the function Deal() to deal card to every player. (not sending)

Step 7

The first player plays first.

Step 8

The function select() gives assistance to determine whether there is a new coming connection or new choice.

Step 9

Waiting for all players to connect with dealer. The screen displays the instruction when all players are connected.

Step 10

If all the players are connected, the game starts, and the monitor displays the game screen.

Step 11

If it is not the dealer’s turn, the dealer is receiving the choice sent from player using the function Hit_Card().

Step 12

After receiving the choice from player, the function Check_Bust is used to examine whether the total point of the player is busted.

Step 13

At the end of checking the total point of the player, the function Check_Turn is used to determine who is going to play the game with the dealer next.

Step 14

At the end of the while loop, the dealer sends all card’s information to all players to update their card status.

Step 15

The dealer keeps doing step 10 to step 14 until it is time for dealer’s turn.

Step 16

After the dealer finishes the turn, the function Check_Winner to analyze who wins the game.

Client

The code for client is a little bit same as the code for the connection between server and one client. The only different is the additional code for chatting with server. The code involves 5 steps.

Step 1

The menu is displayed at the screen in order to get the input of IP, Port and the number of player.

Step 2

Using the function socket() to create a socket to communicate with client.

Step 3

Inserting some value including IP and Port into the struct sockaddr_in.

Step 4

The function connect() helps to send request for connection with server.

Step 5

After receiving acceptation from dealer, the player will wait for the value "startgame = 1" sent from dealer to start the game.

Step 6

After the connection is established, client will receive the card status from dealer until the game end.

Step 7

The game screen is displayed.

Step 8

Using the function Hit_Card() to determine whether the client wants to draw a card. If the client wanted to draw a card, the string “y” is sent to dealer. On the other hand, if the client did not want to draw a card, the string “n” is sent to dealer.
